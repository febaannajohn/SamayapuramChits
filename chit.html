What is a chitty

As per the Chit Funds Act of 1982, a "chit" refers to a transaction where individuals, under various names such as chit, chitty, or kuri, enter into an agreement. In this agreement, each participant agrees to contribute a certain sum of money, typically in periodic installments, over a specific period.

In simple terms, a chitty is an agreement between a Foreman and Subscribers, where each Subscriber commits to paying a fixed amount at regular intervals for a set duration.

Types Of chitty




Long-term chitties, spanning 20 to 60 months, offer lucrative investment opportunities, yielding high dividends. These chitties serve as a secure alternative to loans, enabling customers to fulfill their needs through preplanned installments. They provide a safe avenue for accumulating substantial funds for future requirements."

Spanning 10 to 20 months, suit subscribers seeking immediate prize money. With fewer bidders, early auctioning offers better chances to fetch optimum amounts for emergencies.

KSFE conducts chitties of different durations. Short term chitties ranging from 30 months to 60 months duration are appropriate for subscribers who need the prize money immediately, as there will not be more customers for bidding the chitty for maximum discount and hence better chance for bidding chitty early and the subscribers can auction the chitty for an optimum amount to meet their emergencies.



The Chit Process – How It Works

The chit fund begins on a specified date and continues for a duration equal to the number of subscribers. Each month, members contribute their predetermined monthly installments to the chit fund pot. A reverse auction is conducted monthly, allowing members to bid for the Prize Money.

The member willing to accept the lowest sum with the lowest bid wins the Prize Money for that month. The winning member receives the Prize Money, while the remaining amount is distributed as dividends among all members after deducting the Foreman’s Commission (typically around 5%). This process repeats every month until each member has had the opportunity to win the Prize Money.

Monthly Premium x Duration in Months = Gross Amount

E.g., 5000 * 20 = 1,00,000/- Where 5000 is the maximum monthly contribution needed from a subscriber, 20 is the duration of the chitty in months and 1,00,000 is the maximum sum assured. The duration also equals the number of subscribers, as there must be (not more or less) one subscriber to receive the price money every month.

The chitty starts on an announced date, every subscriber come together for the auction/lot. When there are more than one person willing to take this minimum sum, lot are conducted and the ‘Lucky subscriber’ get the prize money for the month. If there is no person willing to take the minimum sum, then a reverse auction is conducted where subscribers open-bid for lower amounts; that is from 1,00,000 >> 90,000 >> 75,000, and so on.
